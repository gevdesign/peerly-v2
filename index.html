what is peerly

A lifeline programme.
Run entirely by hand.

Peerly is a 1:1 peer mentoring programme for people affected by breast cancer. It matches mentees with lived-experience mentors and supports them through a structured relationship lifecycle.

Every person in the programme has either been through breast cancer themselves, or is going through it now. The programme gives them access to someone who has walked that road — a mentor who can offer perspective, presence, and a connection that clinical support often can't.

Today, the entire operation runs across Gmail, Notion, and Google Sheets. Hannah manually moves people between databases, chases photos, writes matching emails from scratch, and tracks every scheduled follow-up in a spreadsheet. The platform we're building replaces all of that.

project overview

Redesigning how Hannah runs the programme — from five workarounds to one place.

This project designs the admin dashboard that replaces the current manual stack. It gives Hannah a single command centre for onboarding mentors and mentees, reviewing pipeline progress, identifying and confirming matches, and tracking every email in the relationship lifecycle — with SLT sign-off built into the flow.

The goal isn't just efficiency. It's giving Hannah back the hours she spends on copy-paste and status-chasing — so she can focus on what only she can do: making good matches and running a programme that genuinely changes lives.

the problem

Hannah is the system.
That's the problem.

Every mentor and mentee goes through this by hand. No automation. No tooling. Just Hannah, a spreadsheet, and a Gmail drafts folder.

📋

Mentor Onboarding

Copy application form data into Notion by hand. Chase mentors for photos, bios, and cal.com links. Manually vet each candidate with SLT before anyone can be approved.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑

Mentee Intake

Application data entered into a separate database manually before a mentee is even marked ready for matching. No pipeline. No status. Just a row in a spreadsheet.

🔍

Matching

Scan two databases to find a good pair. No recommendations, no capacity signals, no confidence labels — just judgment with no scaffolding and no audit trail.

📧

Email Lifecycle

12+ touchpoints per match, over a full year. Written from Gmail drafts. Sent in weekly batches. Every status tracked in a separate spreadsheet that only Hannah maintains.

build phases

Something valuable
ships at every phase.

Each phase sits on the one before it. You can't match without approved mentors. You can't send lifecycle emails without a match. You can't measure drop-off without email data.

Phase 1 · Foundation

The database + pipeline

  • Mentor pipeline, 7 stages
  • Mentee intake and database
  • SLT approval gate
  • Magic link portal for mentor self-service
Phase 2 · Matching

Recommendations + confirmation

  • Top 3 mentor recommendations
  • Confidence labels + capacity warnings
  • Manual override search
  • Auto-drafted intro email on confirmation
Phase 3 · Email Lifecycle

Automated sends + tracking

  • Automated sends via Resend
  • Editable schedule in admin
  • Open/bounce tracking per email

Analytics + auto-proposals

  • Pipeline drop-off monitoring
  • Email open rate analytics
  • High-confidence auto-match proposals via Slack
  • Mentor load and capacity table
